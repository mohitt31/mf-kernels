Subject: BP1/BP3 comparison: mf-kernels vs MFEM PA + libCEED

Hi Dr. Kolev,

I put together the like-for-like comparison we discussed. BP1 and BP3, 3D hex,
GLL basis with the CEED q = p+2 rule, p = 1..8, on a single AMD EPYC core
(gcc 13.3, -O3 -march=native), against MFEM native PA and libCEED avx/xsmm
blocked, all on the same build. To keep it fair I extended mf-kernels to the full
G^T B^T D B G operator and fed it MFEM's own basis, quadrature data, and element
restriction, so the outputs agree to max 3.8e-15 on the global vector and the
DOF/FLOP accounting is identical across all four.

Short version: against libCEED blocked (the closest match, since both batch
elements into SIMD lanes) mf-kernels is consistently ahead on the
contraction, with the gap narrowing once gather and scatter are included. Against libXSMM, mf-kernels outperforms at lower orders (p<=3) but falls behind at higher orders.
I have not claimed anything beyond that one operator on a structured mesh, and
I have written up the caveats I am least sure about (q = p+2 choice, the shared
GFLOP/s convention, single-core scope) in a flags section.

Write-up and plots attached; full harness and scripts are in the repo at
benchmarks/mfem-comparison. If the methodology looks right to you, the natural
next step is single-socket scaling with a colored scatter. Happy to adjust the
convention if you would rather I compare against a collocated q = p+1 rule.
