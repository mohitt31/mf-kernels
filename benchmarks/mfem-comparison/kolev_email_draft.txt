Subject: BP1/BP3 comparison: mf-kernels vs MFEM PA + libCEED

Hi Dr. Kolev,

I finished the like-for-like comparison we discussed. BP1 and BP3, 3D hex, GLL
basis with the CEED q = p+2 rule, p = 1..8, on a single AMD EPYC 7763 core
(gcc 13.3, -O3 -march=native), against MFEM native PA and libCEED avx/xsmm blocked,
all on the same build. To keep it fair I extended mf-kernels to the full
G^T B^T D B G operator and validated it against MFEM's own output: on identical
input and element restriction the global vectors agree to 3.8e-15 across all orders
and both operators, with the mass-of-ones and stiffness-of-ones invariants matching
exactly, so the DOF accounting is identical across all four.

Result: mf-kernels' blocked AVX2 kernel beats MFEM native partial assembly at every
order on both operators (close to 2x in places), and beats libCEED's own AVX blocked
backend across the whole range. The only thing ahead of it is libCEED with LIBXSMM,
and only at higher order, where mf-kernels trails by roughly 15 to 25 percent; at low
order mf-kernels leads. The even-odd variant does not beat plain blocking once
gather and scatter are included, which fits a partly memory-bound full operator. I
have written up the caveats I am least sure about (q = p+2 choice, the three
different SIMD strategies, single-core scope, the cloud-VM timing) in a flags
section rather than buried them.

Write-up and plots are attached; the full harness, correctness checks, and scripts
are in the repo at benchmarks/mfem-comparison. If the methodology looks right to
you, the natural next step is single-socket scaling with a colored scatter. Happy
to also add a collocated q = p+1 variant if you would rather compare against that.
